The Premise

Your best thinking happens where you can't write it down

You don't need convincing that AI is useful. What stops most leaders is the setup tax — the sense that getting value means learning a stack of agentic tooling before anything comes back. So the easiest first win is the one that asks nothing of you and returns something immediately.

Here it is: your unstructured thinking is where a lot of your value lives — the call you replayed on the drive home, the strategy that clicked mid-run, the thing you finally understood about a person or a deal while doing something else entirely. It's real work. The only reason it doesn't compound is timing: it arrives while your hands and eyes are busy and there's nothing to write on. Remove that one bottleneck and all of it becomes capturable.

Speaking does exactly that. You can already talk these thoughts out — to Claude, to ChatGPT, into a voice note. The question this page answers is what happens after you speak.

The Pattern

Capture → Keep → Draw on

The whole idea in three moves. It's tool-agnostic — you can run a rough version today with a phone and an AI assistant, and a polished version when you want one. What matters is that the loop closes.

01 — CAPTURE

Speak the thought

Hands-free, in the moment it arrives. No typing, no opening a doc — just say it out loud while it's still sharp.

→
02 — KEEP

Turn it into text that lasts

The audio becomes a transcript and is stored — not a voice memo you'll never replay, but searchable words your tools can read.

→
03 — DRAW ON

Put it to work later

Weeks on, ask your AI "what did I think about this?" and it answers from your own words — drafting, reminding, briefing.

Why "Keep" Is the Whole Point

Voice memos & transcripts: must be piped to agentic memory.

Most capture tools stop at step one — you record, and the recording sits unheard forever. The value is entirely in keeping it as text an AI can read, so a passing thought becomes durable context you can summon on demand.

Capture only Lost

  • A folder of voice memos you'll never scroll back through
  • Notes scattered across apps, none of them searchable together
  • The insight is technically saved, but practically gone

Capture + keep Compounding

  • Every thought becomes searchable text in one place
  • Your AI drafts the memo in your own words and framing
  • "What did I say about that pricing decision last month?" gets a real answer

It takes both — they do different jobs

Voice mode is two-way: it's where you actually think a problem through and talk it out. Context Capture is one-way: you speak into it, but it doesn't converse back — it simply pipes what you said into a memory every agent can read. Run them together: converse to think, capture to keep — and the next time you open Cowork or Code to do the work, your own context is already in the room.
