The frame
"The technology is ready. The project is your organization."

The job isn't installing AI. It's closing the gap between what these systems already do and how the business actually runs. Everything below supports that one idea.

1The stakes — the compounding gap

The naive story is "AI makes everyone a bit more productive." The real story is that advantages compound — accumulated memory, context, and redesigned workflows pull fast movers away from slow ones.

2026 is the window. The moat is accumulated context, and you can't backfill it overnight.

2Reset expectations — the J-curve

Most rollouts fail the same way: they bolt AI onto existing workflows, and people feel faster while measurably going slower.

The teams that win redesign the workflow around the agent — specs, review, roles — not drop a tool on yesterday's process. Name the dip early so no one pulls the plug at the bottom.

3The real bottleneck — specification

Models aren't the limit; clear problem-framing is. The new executive skill is problem-finding — knowing which problem deserves solving and specifying it cleanly.

Your judgment and your team's taste become the differentiator the system scales.

4What "agentic" actually means

Not a chatbot. Agents that do multi-step work across your real tools and data — read the source, run the analysis, draft the deliverable, hand back for judgment.

The moat is your proprietary context wired in. Building agents is "80% plumbing, 20% AI" — and the plumbing is where the defensibility lives.

The honest asks

  • 2–3 real workflows with a clear definition of "good," so we can build evaluations — not vibes.
  • Willingness to redesign, not bolt on. Biggest ROI lever; only leadership can authorize it.
  • Pay for the frontier model. Cheaping out is false economy across an agent chain.
  • Patience to iterate. Workflows have a short half-life — ship small, measure, rebuild.

What you get

  • Best people moved off "tokenizable" grunt work onto judgment and relationships.
  • A compounding asset — every workflow accumulates context and grows more valuable.
  • A defensible lead while the window is open.

Two things to land in the room

"AI solves well-specified problems with increasing fluency. Specifying the right problem remains very, very human."

19% slower while feeling 24% faster (METR study) — the J-curve, made real. It reframes the leader's role and makes you the trustworthy guide.

Proof it's real — the "dark factory"

A real security company (StrongDM) ships production software with 3 engineers: agents write, test, and ship; no human writes or reviews code. Humans write the spec, curate test scenarios, watch the scores.

Repo = three markdown spec files; an agent builds against them; a "digital twin" of every external service (fake Okta, Slack, Drive) enables full testing without touching production. Output: a live product, ~16k lines of Rust + 9.5k Go.

The tell: humans didn't vanish — they moved up to spec and judgment. They got there by redesigning everything, not installing a tool. Your path is a climb, not a switch.
