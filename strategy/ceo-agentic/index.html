THE FRAME  —  "The technology is ready. The project is your organization." My job is to close the gap between what these systems already do and how your business actually runs.

The conversation arc

1 · The stakes — the compounding gap

Advantages compound: memory, context, redesigned workflows. 2026 is the window to build the moat — you can't backfill accumulated context later.

2 · Reset expectations — the J-curve

Most rollouts bolt AI onto old workflows: people feel faster while going slower. Winners redesign the workflow around the agent. Say this early so a dip doesn't kill the pilot.

3 · The real bottleneck — specification

Models aren't the limit; clear problem-framing is. The new executive skill is problem-finding. Your judgment becomes the differentiator the setup scales.

4 · What "agentic" means for you

Not a chatbot — agents that do multi-step work across your real tools and data. The moat is your proprietary context wired in. "80% plumbing, 20% AI."

Maturity map — Five Levels of AI Coding

L0 autocomplete · L1 intern · L2 junior dev · L3 dev-as-manager · L4 dev-as-PM · L5 dark factory. ~90% plateau at L2; most stall at L3. The climb is organizational, not technical.

The honest asks

  • 2–3 real workflows with a clear definition of "good" so we can build evaluations — not vibes.
  • Willingness to redesign, not bolt on. Biggest ROI lever; only you can authorize it.
  • Pay for the frontier model. Cheaping out is false economy across an agent chain.
  • Patience to iterate. Workflows have a short half-life — ship small, measure, rebuild.

What you get

  • Best people moved off "tokenizable" grunt work onto judgment & relationships.
  • A compounding asset — every workflow accumulates context and gets more valuable.
  • A defensible lead while the window is open.

One quote to drop

"AI solves well-specified problems with increasing fluency. Specifying the right problem remains very, very human."

One number to drop

19% slower while feeling 24% faster (METR) — the J-curve, made real.

Proof it's real — StrongDM's "dark factory" (the L5 ceiling)

A real security company ships production software with 3 engineers: agents write, test & ship; no human writes or reviews code. Humans write the spec, curate test scenarios, watch the scores. Repo = 3 markdown spec files; an agent ("Attractor") builds against them; a "Digital Twin Universe" (fake Okta/Jira/Slack/Drive) enables full testing without touching production. Output: a live product, ~16k lines Rust + 9.5k Go. The tell: humans didn't vanish — they moved up to spec & judgment. They got there by redesigning everything, not installing a tool. Your path is a climb, not a switch — start small, prove it, ascend the levels.

THE CLOSE  —  "I don't want to sell you a tool. Let's find two or three places where your team's judgment is bottlenecked by busywork, wire agents into your real systems, and prove it with numbers in a few weeks. If it works, we expand. If not, you've spent a little and learned a lot. Fair?"
