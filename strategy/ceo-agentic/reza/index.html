Agentic Systems for REZA — A Working Session

Prepared for Reza Etedali & the REZA Investment Group team. How agentic AI compounds a 30-year relationship business and a ~90,000-name proprietary database — without replacing the judgment that built it.
RATIONAL ROOTKevin Kraliz · for REZA · June 2026
The frame
REZA already runs on a moat most firms will never have: ~90,000 relationships and three decades of deal judgment. Agentic systems compound that edge — they don't replace it.

The technology is ready. The project is fitting it to how REZA actually works — the database, the seven asset categories, the way you solve deal obstacles before they threaten a close.

Where this lands at REZA

01Investor–deal matching at scale

Your "blanketing effect," but targeted and instant: an agent reads a new listing and surfaces the right buyers across the 90k database by mandate, asset type, geography, and capital source — in minutes, not memory.

02Offering memoranda & marketing

Draft the OM, teaser, and pitch package straight from rent roll, T-12, and site data — to REZA's standard and voice — so the team edits and judges instead of assembles.

03Underwriting & comps

Agents pull cap-rate comps, run the model, flag the assumptions that move value — across neighborhood, power, urban infill, net-lease — and show their work for your underwriters to vet.

04Deal-obstacle anticipation

Your edge is "creative problem-solving before obstacles threaten the transaction." An agent monitoring the deal flags financing, title, and tenant risks early — institutionalizing what your seniors do by instinct.

05Buyer fit & intent

Score and rank likely buyers, draft the personalized outreach, and keep the database enriched as the market moves — so no relationship goes cold.

06Market & pipeline radar

Always-on scanning of listings, closings, and capital flows across the Western U.S. retail market — briefing the team on what's worth a call today.

1The stakes — the compounding gap

Advantages compound. The brokerages that wire their database and judgment into agents this year pull away from those who don't — because the moat is accumulated context, and you can't backfill it overnight.

REZA starts ahead. The question is whether that lead widens or gets matched.

2Reset expectations — the J-curve

Most firms bolt AI onto old workflows and people feel faster while going slower. The winners redesign the workflow around the agent.

For REZA that means rethinking how a deal moves from sourcing to close — not dropping a chatbot on the current process. There's a dip before the lift; worth naming up front.

3The real bottleneck — specification

Models aren't the limit; clear specification is. The scarce skill is knowing which problem deserves solving and framing it precisely.

That's exactly what 30 years of REZA judgment is — and it's what makes the system valuable. The agents execute; the taste stays yours.

4What "agentic" actually means

Not a chatbot. Agents that do multi-step work across your real tools and data — read the deal, match the buyers, draft the OM, hand back for your call.

The moat is your proprietary context — the 90k database, the deal history, the REZA Platform — wired in. "80% plumbing, 20% AI," and the plumbing is where the defensibility lives.

The honest asks

  • 2–3 real workflows from REZA's actual pipeline, with a clear definition of "good" so we can measure — not vibes.
  • Willingness to redesign, not bolt on. Biggest ROI lever; it's a leadership call.
  • Pay for the frontier model. Cheaping out is false economy across an agent chain.
  • Patience to iterate. Ship small, measure, rebuild — the half-life of any one workflow is short.

What REZA gets

  • A compounding asset — every workflow makes the database and the firm's knowledge more valuable.

Two things worth sitting with

"AI solves well-specified problems with increasing fluency. Specifying the right problem remains very, very human."

19% slower while feeling 24% faster (METR study) — why "just add AI" backfires, and why the redesign matters more than the tool.

Proof it's real — the "dark factory"

A real company (StrongDM) ships production software with 3 people: agents write, test, and ship; humans write the spec, curate the tests, watch the scores. A repo of three spec files; a "digital twin" of every outside system for full testing without touching production. Output: a live product, ~16k lines of Rust + 9.5k Go.

The tell: the humans didn't disappear — they moved up to spec and judgment, exactly where REZA's seniors already live. And they got there by redesigning the work, not buying a tool.
